Editorial- Food Processing: Harvesting Opporutnities
  • In order to achieve a developed nation's status, it would be elemental for us to enhance the food processing sector
  • We are the largest producer of the following items:
    1. Milk
    2. Pulses
    3. Jute
  • We are the second largest producer of the following crops:
    1. Rice
    2. Wheat
    3. Sugarcane
    4. Groundnut
    5. Vegetables
    6. Fruit
    7. Cotton
  • Food processing involves transforming raw agricultural products into consumable food items, adding value and extending shelf life
  • Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana(PMKSY) aims to create modern infrastructure with efficient supply chain management from farm gate to retail outlet.It includes Mega Food Parks, Cold Chain, and Preservation Infrastructure
  • Mega food parks provide a mechanism to link agricultural production to the market by bringing together farmers, processors, and retailers
  • Some other schemes include:-
    1. Pradhan Mantri Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises(PMFME)
    2. Production Linked Incentive Scheme for Food Processing Industry(PLISFPI)
    3. One District One Product(ODOP)
  • To promote entrepreneurship, a Startup Forum for Aspiring Leaders and Mentors- SUFALAM was held where startups were urged to play a proactive role in collaborating with farmers and venturing into consumer friendly affordable and nutritious products
  • NABARD stands for National Bank for Agricultural and Rural Development
    NAFED stands for National Agricultural Cooperative Marketing Federation
  • Food Processing also involves better health markers on the food packets.
Article 1- Impact of Food Processing on Employment Generation and Skill Development- article by Shaji KV, chairman of NABARD
  • Currently, India is processing less than 10% of its agricultural output, thus presenting immense opportunities for increasing processing levels and leading to immense investment potential in this sector.
  • Status and role of food processing sector in India:-
    1. Contribution to GDP
      • The food processing sector has grown at an annual rate of 8.38% as compared to around 4.87% growth in agriculture and allied sectors.
      • It constituted as much as 10.54% and 11.57% of the Gross Added Value(GVA) in the manufacturing and agriculture sectors
      • The share of food processing in overall GVA has only been 1.88%(2020-21) as compared to 17.86% in manufacturing and 16.26% in agriculture
    2. Employment Generation
      • As per the latest Annual Survey of Industries(ASI), total number of registered workers in food processing sector are 20.32 lakhs
      • The unregistered food processing sector supported employment is 51.11 lakh workers as per the NSSO 73rd round, 2015-16
      • This constituted 14.18% of employment in the unregistered manufacturing sector.
    3. Skill Development Initiatives
      • As per the study undertaken by FICSI, net expected human resource requirement in the 11 major food processing subsectors of the industry in the country; bakery, cold chain, dairy, fish and sea, F&V processing, meat, milling(grain and oil seeds), beverages, ready-to-eat and ready-to-cook, soya processing and spices and condiments during 2021-30 would be around 13.4 lakh.
